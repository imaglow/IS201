Experiential Marketing Campaign

Jan-Apr 2023
A few of my peers and I developed an experiential marketing event for a local business called The Social: an up-and-coming hangout spot for college kids. My team dove deep into the target audience and identified that a party welcoming summer would attract the most college students and increase awareness of The Social. We planned each part of the event using the BETTER Model, including the social media to advertise, and made a highly visual presentation using only Adobe products (InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop). We ended up pitching to the owners of The Social.

Mass Orchestrator Irish Fair of Minnesota

Jun-Aug 2022
I organized the cherished tradition of Sunday morning Mass for 300 people at the Irish Fair of Minnesota. The man who was going to help with Mass dropped out, so I stepped in to plan the logistics for this religious event, which at the time I knew nothing about. I created the program, gathered the symbols, contacted the individuals needed to have Sunday morning mass. It ended up being very well attended and one of the highlights of the participants' weekends at the Fair.

Celtic Corner Manager Irish Fair of Minnesota

Aug 2022
I was in charge of all activities happening in Celetic Corner at the Irish Fair of Minnesota. These activities included two parades, two relay races, a tea party, frequent performances, and on-going crafts for children. Throughout the weekend, there weren't enough volunteers to run all of the activties, so I directed our resources to the places that needed help the most and overall helped children and adults alike enjoy the Irish Fair most. I also found, purchased, and set up 8 yard games for families to play throughout the weekend.

EXDM Talent Show

Apr 2022-Apr 2023
As a teaching assistant, I realized that so many students had interesting talents they don't get to share. So I designed and implemented a talent show for students to participate in for a class assignment. We are working on our 3rd one because students loved it so much. One hundred students attend and on average, 15 students perform.
